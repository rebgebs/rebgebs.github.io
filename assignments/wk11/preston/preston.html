Voted best bakery in town!

By NECIA P. SEAMONS

Michelle Furniss is ready to try her hand at one of her dreams: running a bake shop. Furniss got the business license approved by the Preston City Council for Rae’s Bake Shoppe and Café on March 25. She hopes to open the doors on the new venture by mid April, she said. Furniss has always enjoyed baking, but until now, her creations have been mostly enjoyed by her family of nine children. Two years ago, she took second in the Oneida Stake Academy Heritage Day’s pie contest, at which she won second place for an apple pie. It’s her specialty. Now she’s taking her talent to the public. The bake shop will offer breakfast and lunch, with a menu similar to what Rhema’s offered, except there will be more baked goods and smoothies, she said. To accommodate the additional baking, she has expanded the kitchen area in the location. Pies, cinnamon rolls, cookies, as well as salads, sandwiches, coffee, cocoa, and smoothies will be on the menu. Furniss said she has lived in Clifton since 2015. Before then, she lived in a variety of locations, most recently in Portalnd, Oregon. But she loves the country, and feels like she has “come back home,” to Southeast Idaho. She was raised for a time in Arimo. She still has three of her children living at home, and customers will likely see her 14-year-old daughter, Sarah in the cafe helping out when she can. “She’s really excited about it,” said Furniss.
